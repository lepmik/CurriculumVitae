## **Track record**

I have published 20 articles in peer-reviewed journals, h-index 10, including in high-ranked conference proceedings and journals such as NeurIPS, Nature Communications, Science Advances, and Elife. In addition, I have 5 preprints currently in review. 

My research focuses on the fascinating bidirectional relationship between neuroscience and artificial intelligence: using insights from brain function to advance AI systems while leveraging AI research to deepen our understanding of neural processes. My expertise is fundamentally multidisciplinary, combining computational models and simulations with cutting-edge experimental techniques. 

Since 2020, I have led the BioAI research group at Simula Research Laboratory, having been recognized by this leading institution in the field of scientific computing as a research leader by awarding internal funding for the group. My leadership is effective, and I take my group leader position for such a substantial group of excellent early career researchers seriously and have invested heavily in leadership training through internal and external training. I am deeply committed to fostering an environment where all my team members can thrive and succeed. I have contributed to the careers of early-career researchers, including four MSc students who have pursued a PhD at Simula Research Laboratory, Norway, the University of Oslo, Norway, and Stanford University, US. 

### **Selected publications: Neural cellular automata (NCA)**

Recently, I have developed and investigated Neural Cellular Automata (NCAs), inspired by principles of brain function. This work includes the discovery of emergent attention and scalability in active NCAs \[1\], developmental solutions for abstract reasoning using ARC-NCA \[2\], and modeling memory transfer with EngramNCA \[3\]. These projects form a foundation for the current proposal, and are included although they are currently under review. \[2\] has already been recognized in AI and is featured in the MLST podcast.  
\[1\] Kvalsund MK, Ellefsen KO, Glette K, Pontes-Filho S, **Lepperød ME.** Sensor movement drives emergent attention and scalability in active neural cellular automata. bioRxiv. 2025\. (in review Neural Networks)  
\[2\] Guichard E, Reimers F, Kvalsund M, **Lepperød ME**, Nichele S. ARC-NCA: Towards Developmental Solutions to the Abstraction and Reasoning Corpus. arXiv preprint arXiv:2505.08778. 2025\. (Accepted Alife)  
\[3\] Guichard E, Reimers F, Kvalsund M, **Lepperød ME**, Nichele S. EngramNCA: a neural cellular automaton model of memory transfer. arXiv preprint arXiv:2504.11855. 2025\. (Accepted Alife)

### **Selected publications: Causal inference**

In 2018, together with Konrad Kording, we began research on quasi-experimental methods in neuroscience. The collaboration resulted in one publication and multiple master's theses. This work notably led directly to the methodology for fully mapping out the fruit-fly brain effectome by using our developed technique (Pospisil et al. (2024), Nature, V 634, P 201–209).  
\[8\] **Lepperød ME**, Stöber T, Hafting T, Fyhn M, Kording KP. Inferring causal connectivity from pairwise recordings and optogenetics. PLOS Computational Biology. 2023;19(11):e1011574**.** **20 citations.**
